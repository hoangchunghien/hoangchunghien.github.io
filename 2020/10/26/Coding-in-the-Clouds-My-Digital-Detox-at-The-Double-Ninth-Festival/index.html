Coding in the Clouds: My Digital Detox at The Double Ninth Festival

Posted on 2020-10-26 In Cultural Reflections

Hey fellow programmers!

Taking a break from the digital world, I recently ventured into the heart of tradition, attending the Double Ninth Festival. It's like hitting 'pause' on our daily coding routine and rebooting into a world of family, memories, and cultural heritage.

The Journey Begins

Swapping my usual coding marathons for a real marathon, I embarked on an 8-hour bus journey from the bustling city to the serene Lam Dong Province. The mission? To reconnect with my roots at the Double Ninth Festival, a day dedicated to honoring ancestors in Chinese culture.

A Developer’s Homage

Arriving at my ancestral home, the nostalgia hit me harder than a forgotten semicolon in my code. The agenda was simple yet profound: visit the cemetery, pay respects, and remember the good times. As a Guangdong native, this festival holds a special place in my heart, reminding me of the values and stories that shaped me.

Connection to the Past

The day unfolded like a well-scripted program. My mom and I, armed with traditional offerings, made our way to the cemetery. The path, rugged and untamed, felt like navigating through legacy code – familiar yet challenging. Clearing weeds and setting up the ritual space, I couldn't help but draw parallels between tidying up graves and refactoring code.

Emotional Compile Time

Standing by my ancestors' graves, memories flooded back. I recalled my grandpa's gentle demeanor and his love for coffee – a trait I proudly inherited. It was a moment of reflection, a realization that life, much like coding, is a blend of simplicity and complexity.

Wrapping Up the Ritual

As the incense smoke swirled upwards, blending with the midday breeze, I felt a connection to my heritage stronger than any Wi-Fi signal. It was a reminder that in our fast-paced tech life, slowing down to cherish these moments is crucial.

Returning to the city, my heart was full, and my mind refreshed. The Double Ninth Festival was more than just a traditional observance; it was a journey back to my roots, a reminder to cherish every moment before it becomes a memory. Here's to balancing our tech-driven lives with the richness of our traditions!

Thanks for scrolling through my story, devs. Until next byte!
